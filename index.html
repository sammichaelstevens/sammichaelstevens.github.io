    General Chemistry (BIO1347)

    22 weeks. 22 practicals. Plenty of lectures and drop-ins. Comes with a textbook printed by me and completed by the students themselves. 

    Lockdowns showed that students were at their best with me (struggling with purpose to produce meaningful outcomes) when they were in the lab. This became a key motivator for the creation of this course. Starts with photons and their interactions with atoms and ends with enzymes and their function in organisms. Descriptor here.

    Masters Modules (BIOM549 and NSCM009)

    Bioimaging gives students the opportunity to use a range of microscopy techniques including confocal, AFM, SEM and TEM as well as develop grant application skills.

    Conversely, students on Advanced Laboratory Skills focuses their efforts onto one of three streams working closely with professors on a practical problem. Report writing and poster presenting skills are tested here.
