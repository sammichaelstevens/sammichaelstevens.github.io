Background

I've undertaken research at Oxford, Princeton, Manchester, Stockholm, Daejeon, and Shanghai. My career also took me in other directions including becoming a management consultant, brewer and minster for my sins (pun intended).

My CV TBD

I moved to Exeter in 2015 and became a lecturer in chemistry - in a university without a chemistry department no less! This has afforded me some great challenges recvoling around ching, research, and internationalisation.

Teaching

Current students should look for me on ELE.

All BioChem or BMC students attend my 22 week, 22 practical General Chemistry course (BIO1347). I am very proud of its hands-on nature and appears a great antidote to a loss of coordination and common sense skills in part due to the lockdowns.

At the other end of the student journey, I teach various research skills (including probe microscopies and writing) at the masters-level in the flagship module Advanced Laboratory Skills (BIOM549) and Bioimaging (NSCM009).

A large part of my work relates to internationalisation of the department. I therefore run One Year Study Abroad (BIO3062) and the International Summer School.
